News

We have survived the pandemic. It’s amazing to see you all again. However, we are not out of the woods yet. The last few years have been disastrous for community spaces. Many have closed their doors forever, and times are still tough. Please bear with us while we find our feet again. Our event programme may be a little sparse for a while, as communities that have been dormant wake themselves up. In the meantime, we have ambitious plans to further develop our work and impact:

Apply for 2022-2023 Residency

We are thrilled to announce a brand new one year part-time fully residential course designed to immerse socially-minded mid-career technologists in the knowledge and networks they need to become active agents in society. Expressions of interest for joining the next cohort are now open, and applying is quick and easy. In your time here you will have the opportunity to:

  • Gain a deep understanding of the discipline of political technology and explore its key communities of practice.
  • Develop strong relationships with a close cohort of peers while living together in centrally located college accommodation.
  • Learn from leading expert practitioners working in strategic positions across UK government and civil society.
  • Through a range of lectures, seminars, and reading groups, fellows will learn about the theory behind how our social and political world changes.
  • Create a political technology project with the guidance and mentorship of our faculty.
  • Upon completing the course, you will be invited to join the Newspeak House fellowship, the start of a lifelong relationship with the institution and its networks.

Retrospective

As we emerge from the pandemic, Newspeak House is looking to the future and how it can survive and thrive for many more years to come. A part of this is seeking constructive anonymous criticism from the communities it serves. We will do our best to take heed from any criticisms, wishes and visions that are shared here.

We understand that it can feel hard to give direct feedback, but we really hope that you will, as we genuinely wish to learn, improve and adapt, especially as attitudes and affordances may have changed significantly over the pandemic. We hope to become aware of our blind spots, such that we will be more able to address them.

Plan for the Future

We are building a federated network of residential colleges that serve as the creation, diversification of experimental spaces that have a shared culture of research, experimentation and collaboration. In 2023 we intend to launch two more campuses under the umbrella of The London College of Political Technology that will serve as autonomous sites of study and practice.

The college aims to provide the tools for each campus to self educate, and conduct experiments on themselves that pertain to governance, economics, decision making & finances, such that these spaces are sites of social experimentation. The college provides an umbrella structure such that these learnings are shareable across locations.

If you wish to be involved in this, please reach out.
